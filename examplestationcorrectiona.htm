MENU : Example - Station correction angle

 

Object

To illustrate the method of manually entering survey station and points data and also to calculate the necessary correction angle between 2 stations.

 

In the picture points A and B will be actual stations where the survey instrument will be set up.

Points 1 and 2 will be surveyed from station A and points 3 and 4 will be surveyed from station B.

 

Note:

This example uses the Y and X notation (southern hemisphere) to indicate the co-ordinates.

Enter the data of station A

Activate the survey data base and enter the co-ordinates for station A.

If the first line of the data base is occupied by a stations called KOORD, edit the line and enter the values for stations A.

 

Data base with the standard entry of a KOORD station.

 

Data base after the KOORD entry has been replaced by the actual values of station A.

 

Enter the points surveyed from station A

In this case points 1 and 2 were surveyed from station A. Click on the Points page and enter the data.

Before the Points page is activated select from the Station Configuration dialog box the correct survey format.

 

For this example the format Horizontal distance and Vertical Angle is selected.

 

The Points page after data has been entered for points 1 and 2.

 

Calculate the position of station B

When 2 or more stations are used in a survey, care must be taken to ensure that the co-ordinates and orientations of the stations are correct relative to each other. The best way to achieve that is to measure from the known station to the unknown station each time a new station is used.

 

After points 1 and 2 have been surveyed, take a another measurement to the new position of station B.

Enter the survey data from station A to the position of station B on the points page.

 

The Points page after the data is entered to station B.

 

Note that the code of the B point is entered as .B. The dot (.) in front of the B indicates that it is going to be a duplicate point and that Model Maker should not use this point to build and draw triangles to.

 

The advantage of entering the survey data from A to B on the Points page is that the new co-ordinates for station B is calculated. The co-ordinates of B can now used to calculated the co-ordinates of points 3 and 4.

 

Use the new co-ordinates of B to define the station

The co-ordinates of B is now known. We now have to copy these co-ordinates to the Station page in order to define a new station B

 

Click on the 3rd line, the one on which the co-ordinates of B is calculated. Look at the bottom of the Points page and locate the button. Click the add station button.

 

The new calculated co-ordinates of B will be copied to the Station page and a new station will be defined.

 

 

New station B

Activate the Station page and notice the new station called B

 

The Station page with new station B after using the ADD STATION button on the Points page.

 

Two changes must be made to this page before survey data can be added to station B.

 

1) Remove the (.) in front of the name B. We want to do this because station B, falling directly on the point B, must be used to build triangles as well.

2) The correction angle between stations A and B must be calculated.

 

In the field book (shown at the top of the page) we see that the direction from A to B was measured as 243.26.06

From B to A the direction was measured as 0.00.00 degrees. Mathematically two stations are in orientation if the difference between the 2 directions is 180 degrees.

In this example the difference is 243.26.06-0.00.00=243.26.06 degrees.

Because this is not equal to 180 degrees we must add a correction angle of 243.26.06-180=63.2606 degrees to all the points surveyed from station B.

When the survey points are calculated, Model Maker will automatically add the station correction angle to the horizontal angles of the surveyed points.

 

Correction angle - define manually

If you know what the correction angle of the new station must be, enter it in the column CORR.A or use the automatic method to let Model Maker calculated the angle.

 

 

Correction angle - automatically

You can calculate the correction angle automatically by first clicking on the desired station and then pressing the at the bottom of the page.

 

The following dialog box will be displayed with the information of station B entered as the SETUP @ STATION.

 

In line 1, enter the point name to which the orientation is being calculated to. In this instance the survey is from B back to A. So A is entered in the Name column. The co-ordinates of station A is automatically entered in the columns and a join is calculated between station B and A. This direction value is then placed in the CALCULATED column. The difference between the calculated and surveyed columns produces the result in the correction column.

In this example the direction from B to A was surveyed as zero degrees. If it was any other value, enter the correct angle in the SURVEYED column.

Click on the OK button. The correct angle will be filled in on the Stations page.

 

The stations page with the right correction angle entered for station B.

 

 

Enter the surveyed points from B

Click on the points page and enter the data from the field book.

 

The points page after the field book is entered.

 

As a check, the survey value from B to A is first entered and the calculated co-ordinates compared to the given co-ordinates of station A. Thereafter the survey to points 3 and 4 are entered.

 

 

Note:

This example is used to illustrate the calculation of the co-ordinates and correction angles of stations. No real attention was given to calculating the Z values (elevation) correctly. In a real survey, make sure that the vertical angles are also entered correctly.
