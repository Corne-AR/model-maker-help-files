MENU : Min - vol_Move

 

The gridded depth file, generated by the Terrace cut&fill option, is used and all the fill points are inspected. If any cut point is found close-by, the volume of the fill point will be balanced with the cut point. The distance that the material must move is recorded.

 

The calculation can be done in total or in a user defined area.

 

Total area

 

This process is repeated until all the fill points are leveled or all the cut points have been distributed.

 

Defined area

 

You will be asked to define the area within which the cut points will be inspected and the area for the fill points. This is usually used when you have stock piles or quarries from where the material will be transported to the fill sites.

 

The results of volume/distance may be recorded to the cad and/or to a spreadsheet file.

Use the distance lines generated in the CAD to determine the origin of the cut material and where it will be placed in the fill area. The lines are saved in different layers associated with the movement distance. Use the CAD display option to view selected layers of lines.

 

The movement process can be done in 2 ways:

 

a) Push cut to fill: Each cut point will be inspected and the available material moved to the closest fill points. The process will start with the cut points closest to the fill area and then in turn  work on the cut points further away. Once all the material of a cut point is moved to fill, will the next closest cut point be inspected.

 

b) Draw fill from cut : Here the fill points will be leveled by drawing material from the closest cut points. Each fill point will be leveled completely before going to the next fill point.

 

The spreadsheet file is displayed in the editor.

 

"Distance (m)"

    "From."     "To"    "Volume m3"     " % "

     0.00     20.00       33871.20     17.68

    20.00     40.00       25497.20     30.98

    40.00     60.00       16308.00     39.49

 

The distances are divided into the different distance categories eg 0 to 20, 20 to 40 etc.

The third column holds the actual volume that has to be moved over that distance. The last column indicates the percentage of the material moved within the category

 

After the calculations are done, select if the model must be updated.

 

Do not update model

 

The grid data (cut and fill elevations) will not be changed.

 

Update to new model

 

The grid data will be updated according to the following method.

All the fill points that have been leveled by taking material from the cut points will be reset to zero. All the cut points that have distributed material will be reset to zero.

If the model consisted of an equal volume of cut and fill points, all the points will end up on zero elevation. If material has to be imported, meaning some fill points have not been leveled, some of the fill points will still have elevations on them.

If material has to be exported, some of the cut points will still be elevated.

By using the volume centroid option on these remaining areas you can easily calculate the centroid and volume of the leftover material.
