MENU : Data                (Alt+D)

 

The functions on the data base dialog box are used to manipulate the following items.

               The stations of the active project.

               The points surveyed from these stations

               General editing of the DTM, CAD & STRINGS data. This includes the adding of constants to the co-ordinates, deleting points and printing the data.

 

Stations

The stations page is automatically displayed when the data base dialog box is activated. On this page, use the functions to manage the DTM stations.

 

The header panel

               No                                                The numeric number (position) of the station in the DTM data base.

                                                         A maximum of 100 stations can be added to the active project.

               Name                                                The name of the station which are also used as the code when the station is plotted as a regular point on the screen.

               Tag                                                The tag assigned to the station.

               Y,X                                                The co-ordinates of the position of the station set up. In the northern hemisphere format this would be the eastings and northings co-ordinates.

               Z                                                The elevation of the station.

               Instrument height                The distance from the ground to the eye sight of the survey instrument.

               Correction Angle                                The calculated horizontal angle that must be applied to all the points surveyed from this station to bring them in orientation.

               Number of points                                The number of survey points that belong to the station.

 

Adding stations

Add a new station to the data base by entering the station information on the first available open line in the station editor.

 

In this example the project already consists of three stations. To add another station, click on line 4 and start entering the station name and all the other relevant information.

 

Two different types of stations.

 

               KOORD stations

         A KOORD station is special kind of station that is used to only hold the information of final reduced survey co-ordinates. This type of station is created simply by entering the

               word KOORD in the name field of the editor.

 

               Regular survey stations

         Any other station not called KOORD, is treated as a regulartachy station.

 

When Model Maker is started, a KOORD station is automatically defined as the first station in the data base. This is because a projecthas to have at least one defined station.

 

This is what you will see when the data base is opened with no points loaded or added.

If you have to add survey points consisting of final reduced co-ordinates, continue to the points page and enter the data. The points will be added to the KOORD station.

 

If you have to add actual survey points consisting of distances, directions and vertical angles, change the station name from KOORD to the name of your station and enter the rest of the station co-ordinates.

 

Example of what it would look like after the KOORD station has been renamed and the input of the station data completed.

 

Click the Points tab to start entering the data of the points surveyed from the station. You can also double click on a station line to turn over to the points page.

 

Editing stations

 

A station is edited by simply entering the new information in the editor.

               Editing a KOORD station has no effect on the system. If the name of the station is called KOORD the rest of the fields - tag and co-ordinates - are ignored.

               Editing a tachy station has a much wider effect. Keep in mind that the points surveyed from a tachy station are normally reduced to final co-ordinates based on the distance

               and direction relative to the co-ordinates of the station. When a tachy station is edited, Model Maker will automatically recalculate the co-ordinates of all the points that belong

               to that station.

 

Deleting stations

A station is deleted by pressing the button and confirming the request.

               Keep in mind that deleting a station also implies that all the points that belong to the station, also be removed from the data base.

 

Correction Angle

 

Click the button to recalculate the correction angle of a selected station. The standard correction dialog box will be displayed.

In the correction dialog box, enter the names, co-ordinates and directions surveyed to the trigs beacons that were used for the horizontal orientation correction calculations. Joins (inverses) will be calculated between the station and the trig beacons and the correction angle calculated.

                       After the correction angle of a station is changed, the positions of all the surveyed points belonging to the station will also be re-calculated.
