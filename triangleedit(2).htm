MENU : Tri - Edit

 

1)  It is extremely important to edit triangles correctly.

 

2)  A triangle represents the surface between 3 points, and 2 triangles next to each other represent the surface between their 4 points.

 

3) In the example below there are 4 points numbered 1,2,3 & 4.

 

 

Triangles A and B are formed between them. Should the common leg between the triangles connect points 2 & or 1 & 4?

 

4)  This would depend of the topography of the survey terrain. On a flat to generally undulating terrain, the position of the common leg will have little influence on the model. If the terrain is hilly with steep undulations or other definite breaklines are present in the survey, the position of the common leg very important.

5)  A breakline is the position on the surface where a definite change in slope occurs. The surface as indicated by B has a steep but constant slope. This is apparent from the fact that the contours are evenly spaced and close to each other. The surface as indicated by C is flat, indicated by less contours on the surface. Now the change in slope from surface B to C is depicted by the breakline.    

 

6)  The correct way to model the breaklines is to force (edit) the legs to follow the breakline so they do not to cross over the breakline. Editing triangles to have their legs follow the breaklines are done by various methods.

 

7)  Model Maker allows you to manipulates DTMs with freedom  to perform various tasks, which are most apparent in editing triangles.

 

8) Model Maker allows triangles to be edited before or after creating of the original model. Use any combination of automatic and manual editing methods.
