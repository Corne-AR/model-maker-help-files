IRRIGATION : Proposed pump pressure

 Top  Previous  Next

 

This dialog is used to determine the approximate pump pressure that will be needed to run the system. The proposed pump pressure is a value based on the design margins as specified by the operator, the elevation differences between the pump and various blocks and the pressure required at the block valves.

 

After you have selected the design margins and units, click the Calculate button. A summary of the results of all the blocks is shown in the block report.

The estimated pump pressure required for each block is displayed and the critical block, the one which required the highest pump pressure, is shown in the critical block summary.

If you want the system to assign the calculated pump pressure to the pump node, click the OK button. Irri Maker will then proceed to calculate the desired pipe diameters assuming the pump delivers the selected pressure.

 

For the purpose of calculating the % losses, the initial pump pressure is assumed to be the required pressure of the critical valve + the height difference between the valve and the pump.

In the example in the above dialog image, the initial pump pressure is 17m+38.47m = 55.47m

 

1)       Head losses in mainline pipes

                                                            i.     % = a percentage of the initial pump pressure.

                                                            ii.     m = A fixed pressure value

                                                          iii.     Length = A fraction of the total pipe length between the pump and the critical valve.

                                       The entered value is the pressure loss in the pipe (meter) per 100m of pipe length.

                                       Example. Length : Pump to critical valve = 650m.  User entered value is 2m.

                                                         Value used in calculation = 650/100*2 = 13m pressure loss.

b.       Head losses in fittings

                                                            i.     % = a percentage of the initial pump pressure.

                                                            ii.     m = A fixed pressure value

c.       Head losses in valves

                                                            i.     % = a percentage of the initial pump pressure.

                                                            ii.     m = A fixed pressure value.

d.       Design Margin

                                                              i.     % = a percentage of the initial pump pressure.

 

 

NOTE:

 

The suggested pump pressure is based on 5 main components:

 

Theory:

1) The required pressure in the critical valve.

2) The elevation difference between the pump and the valve.

3) The head losses in the pipes as suggested by the user.  ( % , fixed value or % of distance)

4) The head losses in the valves and fittings. (% or fixed value)

5) The design margin. (%)

 

This in only the theory. When the system is calculated hydraulically the following elements will determine the actual head losses.

 

Actual:

1) The valve pressures.                As required by the individual blocks.

2) The elevation differences.        As calculated between the pump and individual blocks.

3) Head losses in the pipes.                As calculated by the actual flows, pipe diameters and friction factors.

4) Head losses in the fittings.        As determined by the secondary K values associated with each node and entered in the node database.

 Note: The secondary losses on the calculation page must be checked.

 

5) The design margin will be the actual difference between the valve required pressure and the actual pressure.
