MENU : Irr - Global Reports - Water Hammer

 

 

 

Note: The most widely used version of the Water Hammer formula is in Imperial measurement units.

Model Maker will therefore convert all units internally to Imperial, in order to obtain a report on whether the system are exposed to Water Hammer occurrences.

 

When designing an irrigation system, it is common for Water Hammer occurrences to appear at certain points (valves), when those valves are closed for specific times.

 

Example from the image above:

 

1) The flow velocity in the pipe connected to the valve.

 

2) The length of all the pipes between the valve and the pump or the valve and a previous valve.

 

3) The time in seconds to close the valve.

 

4) The pressure at the valve.

 

By taking 2 seconds to close Valve 2 the pressure  throughout the pipe that connects to the pipe will increase to 101.16.

An * is added when this increased pressure is larger than the pipe's design pressure.

The increase in pressure will get lower as the duration to close the valve gets longer.

From the above example, taking 8 seconds to close the valve will only raise the pressure on the pipe to 27.71. This is still larger than 12 and an * is still displayed.

 

After running this report, it is the users responsibility to:

 

·resize the pipes manually so pipes pipes have lower flow velocities
·change pipe classes to have larger design pressures

 

in order to avoid Water Hammer occurrences.
