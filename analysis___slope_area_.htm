MENU : Ana - sArea                                

 

This function calculates the surface area of the model within a defined area.

Model Maker uses a method whereby the area is internally divided into little squares. The slope at the centre of each square is determined from the triangle model and the slope area of each block is then calculated. The slope areas of all the squares are accumulated to calculate the slope area of the defined area.

The grid points are plotted while the function is in progress allowing you to see the distribution of points and determine if the selected interval is satisfactory.

For accurate results choose a small square interval.

 

This function is normally used to calculate the slope area of a landscape terrain allowing you to determine the amount of topsoil or grass that must be planted.

 

EXAMPLE

This method has the following assumption.

1) The grid interval is small enough so that the slope value at the grid point is representative of the full grid block size.

2) That the areas not covered by short blocks (light blue dots) are balanced out by the over areas (red dots)

 

The 2 assumptions are therefore very related to the grid size.

 

Based on the shape of the area in the above example it seems like there are more over areas (red) than under areas (blue). It is therefore necessary to use a relative small grid interval to minimize any potential assumption errors.

 

 

 

EXAMPLE:

 

This function calculates the surface area of the complete modelof the active project. The process traverses through all the triangles, calculating the slope and slope area of each one. The total of the triangle slope area is the slope area of the whole model.

 

Hints:

Use the triangle editing routines to prepare the triangle model before the area is calculated. Apply the SIDE option to make sure that the area covered by the triangles is correct.
