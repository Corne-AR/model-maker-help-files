TRIANGLE : Edit - Summary

 Top  Previous  Next

 

Triangles editing is important on models with steep slopes, roads, embankments, walls and other features that make breaklines come into play. Even terrain can be modeled   without editing.

 

The number of triangles that need to be edited depends on the terrain, the number of survey points and the specific position of these points.

 

Decide to use a specific editing method based on your editing experience and the terrain.

 

Only consider using the method where the CAD lines following the breaklines are drawn and then Model Maker searches for triangles to comply to these lines using the  NORMAL SEARCH  method, in the following cases.

 

a)        The CAD lines indicating the breaklines are available, ie. dxf, or need to be on the contour plan for visual purposes.  If you need to add        all the breaklines just to make the triangles follow the correct pattern, consider a different approach.

 

b)        The points have been coded in such a way that would make it easy to enter the CAD lines.

 

Most of the time, allow  MM  to find triangles with the  QUICK SEARCH method. Then, inspect the model by drawing the contours and then manually edit the necessary triangles that interpolate the contours incorrectly.

 

Even if you use none of the automatic editing facilities, you can use the CAD lines in a very informative way. While editing triangles manually, look for any CAD line still visible after the triangles are drawn.

 

This is an indication that no leg of any triangle lies on top of the CAD line, meaning that one or more legs of the triangles are crossing the CAD line. Edit the triangle until a leg of a triangle falls on top of the CAD line.

 

Save the triangles after they have been edited.
