MENU : teR - Cut-fill                                

 

Model Maker has powerful volume calculation capabilities found on the Terrace menu. These methods calculate the volume between 2 surfaces loaded into any of the survey projects.  Before this option is used, ensure that the ground or original model is loaded in project 1 and the design or latest model is in project 2.

 

The following functions may be performed on the models:

 

 See bottom of this topic

 

 

 

Ground Project

 

Use this button to specify which of the projects is the design and ground surfaces.

 

The flat surface option can be used to simulate a surface at the desired level without creating a physical dtm surface. If the flat surface @ elevation option is checked, Model Maker will compare the elevations of the design project to that of the defined elevation of that flat surface and not to that of the dtm model in the ground project.

 

 

The volume is calculated between the 2 models using sections.

 

 

Elevations on both the design and ground models are calculated at the specified grid interval (square size). The difference in elevation between the 2 models is determined at each grid point and a volume for that grid is calculated by multiplying this difference (depth) with the square of the grid interval. These prisms of volumes are accumulated to produce the final volume. Always select the grid interval as small as practically possible to produce the most accurate result.  

 

Topsoil

Enter the topsoil depth in millimeters. The depth will be subtracted from the ground model before the cut & fill between the ground and design surfaces are calculated. The volume of topsoil will be shown separately on the printout.

 

 

What this diagram shows is that the layerwork depth will be subtracted from the design profile, the topsoil depth will be subtracted from the ground surface and the cut & fill volume will then be calculated between the remaining (lowered) surfaces of the design profile and the ground level.

 

Layerworks

Enter the layer works depth in millimeters. The depth will be subtracted from the design model before the cut & fill between the ground and design surfaces are calculated. The layer works volume will be shown separately on the printout.

The grid points will normally be calculated in a west-east direction.

 

Note:

As from version 2.43, Model Maker calculates both the topsoil and layerworks areas based on the slope area and not just the flat footprint area as in the previous versions. This means that if both 100mm topsoil is removed and 100mm layerworks added there is a good chance that the volumes will differ. The reason being that the surface area of both models are not exactly the same although the flat footprint area of the excavation will most probably be identical.

 

Under certain conditions the volume calculation is more accurate if the grid points are taken at an angle over the model. Change the grid angle to, for example, 30 degrees, if your data is very theoretical, meaning that the points of either the ground or design models are on regular grids or on lines going exactly east-west or north-south. Even if your data is in a random format, it is a good check to calculate the volume at an angle of 0 as well as any other angle between 1 and 44 degrees. Using different angles should give you a double check on the volume.

As a further check, always compare the cut and fill areas after the volume calculation with the actual area of the design model area.

The grid points can be saved in an ASCII file. The elevation of these points consist of the depth value between the two models. Use this data to generate contours of depths. This file is also used in the mining module to predict the movement of material between the cut and fill areas of the terrace.

During the calculations, the grid positions will be colored red if in cut and white if in fill.

The cut and fill areas may be highlighted by generating CAD elements on the grids.

 

 

 

This calculation can be done within a user specified polygon area.

 

This function works on the same principles as the regular grid volume calculations. The purpose is to determine the constant value with which the design data must be adjusted to obtain a cut and fill balanced design. It is recommended that this is done before toe lines are determined because toe points are not adjusted. The original elevations of the design will be used to calculate a first run volume. If the cut volume is more than the fill, Model Maker will lift the design surface by a certain amount and the volume will be recalculated. The design will be lowered if the fill is more. The process will be repeated until the cut equals the fill volume. Model Maker will display the amount to be added or subtracted from the design surface to achieve balance. You may specify a compaction factor to apply during the calculations. For example - 0.15 factor means 1m3 cut = 0.85m3 fill. The constant value may automatically be added to the design elevations after the it is reported.

 

Three functions may be used.

 

All the triangles        : The design triangles are treated as a single surface. The cut & fill will be determined across the                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          whole model and a single new difference in elevation presented. Use this method to balance                                                                                                                                                                                                                             the whole design.

 

All the islands        : Each set of island triangles are calculated separately and an updated elevation is determined                                                                                                                                       for each. This method is useful when the design consists of many  individual platforms, each                                                                                                                                                                          with elevations independent of the each other.

 

Indicate island        : The same as above, but indicate the island that must be balanced.

 

Definition of an island:

This picture shows the design triangles for 4 housing platforms. This will be treated as 4 individual islands and each may be balanced individually. An island is a set of triangles where each one is a neighbor of at least one other triangle in the island. The boundary of the island is defined by the triangles on the side having no other neighbor.

 

This function will place new DTM points on the neutral line between the cut and fill surfaces.

This method of calculation will determine the depths perpendicular to the surface of the design model.

The difference between the regular grid method and the thickness check is:

 

a) Grid calculates the volume between 2 surfaces by looking at the vertical depth between the models.

 

b) Thickness check does not do volume calculations but only determines the average thickness (perpendicular to the design model) between 2 surface. This is often used to check if layer works on  batters (especially concrete) are done to the desired thickness value.

Result Output:

If project #3 does not have any dtm points, the option will be available to have the result copied as dtm point to project #3.  The elevation of the new points will be equal to the perpendicular depth difference between the 2 surfaces.

The thickness can also be copied to cad as regular text.
