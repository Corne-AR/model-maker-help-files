MENU : Tri - Del

 

Delete a triangle by pointing to the inside of the triangle.

Use the side option is to see if there are any gaps in the triangle model.  

 

Deleting triangles is just as important as creating and editing them. Contours and cross-sections can only be produced from triangles, meaning that you cannot delete contours as such, but you can delete the triangle that produces the contours in a specific area. For example, if you have a surveyed building and  Model Maker draws contours that you don't want through the building, you should delete the triangles covering the building area.

Another example is model that includes a very steep embankment resulting in a high concentration of contours. Deleting the triangles on the embankment prevents the contours from being drawn.

 

Triangles may be deleted individually or in groups. Delete individual triangles with the  DEL  function. Place the cursor inside the triangle and then press ENTER. The triangle common to the deleted one will now have open sides.

 

Mass deletion of triangles falls under the WIPEOUT menu.
