MENU : Pnt - Special - move_Tri

This specialized function moves a point and changes the elevation based on the slope of an indicated triangle.

Often when measuring steep faces of open mine pits, rubble falls down the high wall and obscures the actual position of the toe point of the high wall. Figure 2 shows a typical section of such a situation.

T is the top of the high wall and can easily be measured.

Z is the correct toe position of the high wall but cannot be reached due to the rubble at the foot. (Green lines)

C is a point on the rubble.

Figure 1 shows the layout in plan. The triangle BTC has a slope value of X in the direction of the arrow.

Fig. 1

Indicate the DTM point that must be moved. (Our intention is to move the surveyed point C to the theoretical position Z) After the point is indicated (Point C), indicate the triangle of the slope. (Triangle X) After you have entered the value of the elevation of point Z, Model Maker will calculate the plan distance that point C must move in the direction of the slope of triangle X based on the height difference between C and Z.

Fig. 2

The function has a limited application but can be powerful when applied to the right situations.
