MENU : gXt - Utility - More - Quick_dam

 

Quickly develop an earth dam profile and calculate the various volumes and other information.

 

You must have the survey of the dam site and upstream area where the water is going to be, loadedin project 1.

The triangles must also be loaded.

 

Next, add a general string on the position where the dam wall is going to be.

 

 

The elevation of the dam wall string is not important. It is only used to define the position of the centre line. Start the string on the right side of the stream when looking down stream. This is necessary because Model Maker will assume that the upstream side of the dam wall will be to your left when looking along the centre of the dam wall from the right side of the stream.

 

Quick dam wall dialog box

 

The dam wall dialog box consists of 3 major pages. The parameter page is entered first.

 

 

Elevations & widths

Water level

 

This is the full dam water level and is used for calculating the water capacity and surface area.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Wall - Crest level

 

This is the level at the top of the dam wall. If a zero curvature height is entered will the crest level be taken as horizontal for the total length of the wall.

 

Wall - Crest width

 

The width of the wall at the crest level.

 

Wall - Curvature

 

The amount that the wall will be higher in the centre than on the edges.

 

Key - Width

 

The width of the keycut at the bottom.

 

Key - Depth

 

The depth of the keycut below the ground.

 

The spillway parameters are not used in any calculation. It is only printed in the final report.

 

 

Design slopes

 

These are the values indicating the steepness of the wall and keycut sides.

 

 

After the parameters are entered, click on the CALCULATIONS page for the next input.

 

 

 

The only two inputs on this page are:

Dam Wall sections

The chainage distance at which Model Maker will extract cross-sections at right angles to the centre line string. These sections would then be used as the ground line on which the dam wall profile, as defined by the parameters, will be placed. The area of the dam wall profile at every interval will be determined and the volume calculated by end area method.

The size of the interval may be any value, but should be small enough to generate at least more than 10 sections along the wall. A small value produces more sections and should be more accurate.

 

Water volume - Grid size

This dictates the accuracy of the water area and volume calculations. Again, use a practical, small grid. However, if a grid that is too small, the program will take a long time to finish the calculations.

 

Click on Wall to calculate the wall volume and on water to calculate the water volume.

 

Check the needed boxes if the results must be copied to strings.

 

 

Calculations of:

Wall

Click on this button to calculate the dam wall volume. Indicate the dam wall center string. Indicate the string at the position of the wall.

Sections will be extracted along the triangle surface of the active project. The profile will be placed on each section and the fill area calculated. The volume of the key cut is also calculated.

The sections will be displayed on the graphics screen only. No points or cadlines are added to the database.

Figure showing temporary images of calculated wall profiles.

 

Calculations of:

Water

Model Maker will ask you to indicate the approximate water area. Within this area, the program will calculate the surface elevations at the regular interval as specified  Any ground elevation above the water level will be ignored. Elevations below water level will be added to the dam capacity. These points will be marked with a white cross indicating the extend of the water surface. The user defined area should thus be larger than the actual water area except at the wall itself. Indicate the waterline as close as possible.

 

 

Calculation results

Wall

The wall volume, length and deepest point is calculated.

Water

The surface area, volume and deepest point is calculated.

These values are used in the QUOTES page of the dialog box.

 

Quotes

 

Client Info

Enter the client's information on this page. This information is displayed on the printer quote.

Prices

Enter the relevant unit prices for the earth moving and diesel.

General

Enter the contractor's name and any other remarks.

         

Use the calculate button to recalculate the totals after changes have been made to the unit prices.

Finally, print the quote when done. The quote is printed to the text editor where you can save it, alter it or print it to a printer.

 

The Open and Save buttons are used to load and save the relevant dam parameters.

 

 

Calculate the quarry area needed to excavate fill material for the dam

 

 

 

 

 

Use the parameters on the Quarry page to calculate the precise area from where the fill material will be excavated to build the dam wall.

 

The volume value will normally be the amount calculated when the wall was designed on the Calculations page.

 

The function will determine the area assuming the depth will not be more than the maximum specified.

 

The area will assume the defined side slope when close to the bounding string.

 

 

Before doing the Calculate area, you need to draw a general string string that will define the area of the proposed quarry.

 

This area will normally start close to the stream up toeline of the wall and extend along a contour height away from the wall.

 

If the area is too small to get the full volume within the maximum depth, then the depth will be increased.

 

Normally you would define one side of the area bigger than what is needed, the calculation will then stop short of that boundary, when the needed volume is reached.

 

See next image for more detail.

 

 

 

 

 

 

When the calculation begins, you need to indicate a starting point.

 

From this point the program will work in a circular mode to find points within the indicated boundary string to get the material from.

 

When enough points within the defined area is checked to make up the volume, the sequence stops.

 

In the example, notice the part of the area that is not needed since the required volume (at the maximum depth) is reached.

 

If the complete area has been used and the volume not reached, the depth will be increased and the process started again.

 

Once the process is completed, draw a new string or cad line around the red crosses to record the area.

 

Remember, the crosses are just temporary and will be removed after a Replot.
