End of spans pipe pressure:

 

This is the theoretical calculated pressure at the end of the pivot based on the start flow and pressure.

 

In this example the pivot pressure, top of pipe is 27m and the pivot flow is 78.5 l/s.  This flow is then distributed through the pivot spans on an circular, even basis.  Based on the pipe diameter and friction, the available pressure in the spans decrease until no flow is left and the pivot end is reached.

 

The pressure in the system that is then still available at the pivot end is reported as End of spans, main pipe pressure.

 

On a level surface, this will then also be the lowest pressure in the pivot.
