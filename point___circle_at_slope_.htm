POINT : Circle at slope

Top  Previous  Next

 

MENU : Pnt - sLope

 

Add a circle from a DTM point. This circle will represent the possible positions that any point may have if the new point is at a set elevation and set slope from the original point. The radius of the circle is thus calculated as the elevation difference between the 2 points times the entered slope. The circle is drawn in CAD with the default parameters.

 

1) Single point

A single circle representing the top or bottom edge of the embankment will be drawn from a single indicated point.

 

2) Between 2 indicated points

Multiple circles will be drawn between the 2 points.

Circle at slope between 2 indicated points

 

Add circles along a line as indicated by 2 user defined points. These circles will represent the possible positions that any point may have if the new point is at a set elevation and slope from the line. You will be requested for the platform design elevation of the 2 points

 

The radius of the circles is then calculated as the elevation difference between the new point and the elevation on the line at that position, times the entered slope.

An example showing slope circles around 4 points. The blue lines are the proposed toepoint positions and the red circles define the position where the design terrace edge would be.

 

The green line (simulating the design platform) was drawn in manually by placing it approximately at a position along the circles.
