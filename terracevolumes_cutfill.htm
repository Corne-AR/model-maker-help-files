Use this option to calculate the volume of dams or other structures that rise to a specific water level.

 

User defined depths

 

Specify up to 9 interval values  to add additional information to the report of the volume results.

These intervals have the following meaning.

 

FIG A Cross-section showing a typical cut&fill

 

In addition to the total volume, the specified intervals will also tell you what amount of material was cut within a depth of intervals 1,2,3 and 4 and filled within intervals 1,2 and 3. The interval depth is taken along the ground surface each time, and not taken horizontally like contours.

 

Specify 9 intervals on which you would like a breakdown of the volumes or define a single constant interval. Using the constant interval, Model Maker will report the volume within each defined layer.

Note:

If a topsoil depth is specified the cut and fill volumes are calculated between the design model and the adjusted ground level.

The adjusted ground level being the original ground level minus the topsoil thickness.

 

Contour Volume

 

 

A new index elevation setting has been added. In the past this value was always 0 and as an example an 8m contour interval will report in elevations of 0,8,16,24 etc.

Now you can change the index to a user defined value .

For a user value of eg. 101 the reporting will be in intervals of 8m starting not from 0 but from 101 up or down.

 

The volumes within contour layers may also be presented. These volumes are typically used in the calculations of slimes dams.

 

FIG B Section showing typical contour elevations

 

Unlike the depth interval report (FIG A) the volumes within contour layers takes horizontal slices through the models and produce a report of the volumes between relevant contour values.

 

This feature can be used very effectively to calculate the volume of a dam that has a complex shape or has islands within the full water mark level. All you need to do is to develop a flat surface with the elevation equal to the full water level.

Use this model as your design and calculate the cut & fill between it and the original model. Once the volume is calculated, ignore the cut volume. The fill volume will be the capacity of the dam since it represents the amount of material (water) that needs to be added to the original model to fill it up to the design surface.

Vertical Volume

 

This volume report is a variation of the contour report. Where the contour report takes horizontal slices through the model, the vertical report looks at vertical columns of volume.

 

In this diagram, the red surface represents the ground and the green surface is the design.

 

If the vertical volume is reported in depths of 5m and the design model is at maximum 10m below the ground then the volumes will be reported in 2 categories.

 

A) 0 to 10m : The column of volume (cut) where the design is deeper than 5m below the ground.

 

As soon as the design exceeds the 5m interval, all the volume from 0 to 10m is reported.

 

B) 0 to 5m : The total volume (cut) where the design is between 0 and 5m below the ground.

 

1) This diagram shows the cut situation. The same applies for the fill where the design is above the ground surface.

 

2) This type of volume reporting is often used where the contractor gets paid by total material excavated from zero to certain depths categories.
