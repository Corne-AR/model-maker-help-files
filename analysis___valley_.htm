MENU : Ana - Valley                                

 

This function inspects the complete model and analyzes the triangles to locate the valleys and ridges and to show the natural water courses and watersheds. Arrows will indicate the slope direction for water courses.

 

 

Valley / Watershed lines

Pen number        : Click on the coloured box to go to pen colours.  Select colour you require.

Layer name        : Places lines on this layer.

 

If you choose to copy the result to CAD, the lines showing the valleys and watersheds will be added to CAD as regular elements.

 

Hints:

Model Maker uses the following algorithm to inspect all the triangles and indicate the natural features.

A valley is formed where two triangles meet and their natural slopes face each other.

Oppositely, a watershed is created when two triangles meet and their slopes face away from each other.

 

EXAMPLE:

 

Note the arrows pointing away from the watershed (white) and towards the valley (blue).

A regular slope (two joining triangles with slopes in the same direction) will not produce any lines.

Because this exercise is theoretical, water courses are not always accurately shown as they occur in nature.
