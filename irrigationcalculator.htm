This calculator page consists of 2 section.

 

Easy Calculator

 

This function allows you to enter any 2 of the following values. Flow, Diameter or Velocity. The third variable is always calculated based on the values of the other two. The velocity is calculated in the above example.

 

To calculate the required pipe diameter for a certain flow and velocity, check the diameter option and enter the other 2 values.

 

In summary. Only two values have to be entered and the third one is calculated automatically. You do not have to press a button for the calculations to take place.

Also, the easy calculator is not dependant on the more complicated length, diameter and friction calculator.

 

Length, Diameter and Friction calculator.

 

This section of the calculator allows you to calculate maximum pipe lengths and minimum pipe diameters for a given flow and allowable pressure losses.

A number of variables must be filled in before the calculations can be done.

 

 

You may either select terminal pressures or maximum head losses. In this example the total available pressure in the system is 29 meters. If the maximum losses allowed had been selected the system has 4 meters of pressure available.

 

 

If you want to incorporate a slope percentage in the calculations, enter a value other than zero in this edit box. A zero value will assume the pipe is on a flat surface.

If the water is running downhill, enter a positive slope value. (downhill slope gains pressure)

For a system pumping uphill, enter a negative slope value (uphill slope looses pressure)

 

If you have a dtm loaded and want to calculate the slope between the start and end point of the pipe (being analyzed), use the interpolate option and indicate the 2 points. The program will calculate the slope based on the distance and elevation difference between the 2 indicated points.

 

There are 2 options when specifying the pipe flow.

 

 

Flow at regular spacing along the pipe.

 Here you enter the flow of each emitter spaced at the defined regular interval. In the above example each emitter uses 3m3/h and they are spaced 2 meters apart.

 

In this example there is a constant flow of 150m3/h  through the pipe

 

Calculations

 

There are 3 different kinds of answers you can get from the system.

 

Maximum pipe length

 

Enter the diameter of the pipe you are analyzing and press the calculate button. In the above example it is calculated that a 125mm pipe having a starting pressure of 30 meters and an end pressure of 1 meter and conveying 150m3/h, can be 367meters long.

Notice that the Easy calculator is used to calculate the velocity. When the calculate button is pressed, the flow and pipe diameter are entered in the appropriate edit boxes in the easy calculator and the velocity calculated.

 

Minimum Pipe Diameter

 

 

Use this option to calculate the required pipe diameter to convey water over a certain distance.

In this example a 32mm pipe can be 80 meters long, having emitters at every one meter down a 1% slope and burning up 4 meters of friction losses. The velocity at the start of the pipe is 2.17m/s

 

The total losses option is the inverse of the previous 2 methods.

In this format you enter the length of pipe and pipe diameter and the program calculates the total losses for you.

In this example the elevation gain (going downhill) is 0.8 meter and the friction loss is 4.76m resulting in a total of approximately 4 meters.
