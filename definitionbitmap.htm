DEFINITION : Bitmap

 Top  Previous  Next

 

A bitmap is the representation of a digital image consisting of a set of points on a regular grid that defines the colour on the image at all the various positions.

Any picture taken by a digital camera is stored in a bitmap format.

 

Bitmap vs Vector

When regular CAD lines are added to a drawing, they are treated as vectors. Each line has a start and end co-ordinate and when required to draw itself, the pixels on the screen between the two end co-ordinates will be filled in to show the body of the line.

These lines can be added, deleted and edited at will because each one is treated as an individual entity.

 

A bitmap on the other hand only has a top left co-ordinate and bottom right co-ordinate. The body of the bitmap is then filled in by mapping all the grid colours (pixels as defined in the bitmap file) onto the screen or printer canvas.

The bitmap is one entity and although the final image can define a complete picture of CAD lines and other images, those elements do not exist individually. It is only the arrangement of the pixels within the bitmap that creates the visual effect of the different elements.

To edit a bitmap one has to manipulate each of the pixel colours that the bitmap is comprised of. Programs like Paintbrush and other photo imaging tools can be used for that.

 

Bitmap Display

The function to load and display bitmap images is found in the CAD Bitmap menu.

 

Use Of Bitmaps

1) Load and display the bitmap image of a regular digital photograph.  This could be a picture of the site that you have surveyed or any other relevant item.

2) Add the aerial photograph of the terrain to your contour plan.

3) Use a bitmap of the company logo in your title block.

4) Import any other scanned image onto your drawing. Use it to trace over and convert the desired features into vectors.

5) Put a picture of yourself on the contour plan.

 

When using the Quick 3D option and the image is copied to CAD, the resulting image is added to CAD as a bitmap. You can then use the bitmap functions to position the image.
