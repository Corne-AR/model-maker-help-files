MINE : Rehab - Average elevation

 Top  Previous  Next

 

Average elevation is used to rehab an area based on the elevations of the grid points and not the slope of the triangles like most of the other rehab functions. A percentage of volume could be specified to move from the high to the low points. The High and low points are defined based on an average elevation of the points determined with the "Grid Volume" method, in the Volume calculation of Model Maker.

Grid Volume method:

The Grid Volume is calculated based on this method.

 

For each grid point, radials are used to determine the shortest distance between the coordinates of the grid point and the perimeter of the model.

In this example the radial (Red circles) gives the shortest distance between the grid point and the opposite model perimeter.

Based on the elevations where the radial intersects with the perimeter, an elevation is interpolated at the grid point.

The difference between the elevation on the model and the interpolated point from the perimeter lines is then used to calculate the volume.

The differences between the model elevation and the interpolated elevation are then accumulated to determine the dump volume.

This will be the method we use to determine a base of the Average Elevation method in the rehab.

 

1.What the program does is it rotates the model on the elevation of the average of the grid points on the base of an Area that you put a string around.

2. Then the difference between the average and model grid points on the Base is Calculated The" X" value above image.

3.Now we use the Grid points of the model and incorporate the "X" points to get a value "Y"
4.Now we determine the average of the new model, based on the "Y" points above, which will be the "Z" value.
5.The difference between Y and the  Z = Q

6.  On the Q value we now apply a percentage of volume we want to move, this becomes the "L" Value.

70% of Q = L. The 70% will then be applied on all the point elevations. The high points will be 70% lower and the low points 70% higher.

7.  Once this elevation is calculated all the elevations are brought back to be in relation with the normal models elevation, by incorporating the "X" and "Y" values above, on the new elevation that the percentage calculation was done on.

8.  The Program will then prompt you with volumes of witch the above percentages was used for the calculation.

 

This option will give the result based on the initial percentage value of 70%

The cut volume is based on 70% of the total cut and the fill volume is based on 70% of the total fill.

End result. 30% of total cut and 30% of total fill remains.

This option will Balance the volume. This function will always use the least volume either cut or fill to balance.

End result. 70% of the void will be fill and that volume will be subtracted from the cut volume.
