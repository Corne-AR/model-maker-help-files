MENU : Irr

 

This page is designed to assist in the evaluation of the project. You can determine which nodes and pipes do not comply to the design standards, check the average performance and even copy the information to the DTM part of Model Maker where it can be modeled by means of triangles and contours and 3D's of the relative information can be drawn.

The info page will only be active after the calculations on the result page has been performed.

 

 

Time to fill

This information banner is shown when the hydraulics of a regular block is calculated.

 

Velocity        : This time is calculated based on the flow velocity in all the pipes.

The route between the valve and the furthest node is determined and based on the length of each pipe segment and the flow velocity in the segments, the total time it will take a drop of water to travel from the valve to the last node, is determined.

This value can sometimes be a bit misleading. Since it looks at the flow velocity in all the segments from the valve to the last node, if any segment is oversized, for example a manifold pipe feeding only a few laterals, the velocity in the manifold segment can be very low resulting in an abnormal long time to fill that specific branch of pipes.

 

Volume        : Here the total volume of all the pipes are calculated and based on the regular flow volume through the valve into the system, a time to theoretically fill all the pipes is calculated.

 

Looking at the above 2 methods to calculate the fill time of the block one would consider the velocity method to be very conservative and the volume method to be very optimistic. Based on your needs you might pick one of the 2 methods or even an average.

        Flow           -    The amount of water being released by                                                                       the node. (Valves / Sprinkler)

For the pipes the following can be checked:

 Flow            -    The amount of water being conveyed by                                                                     the pipe.

 Pressure    -   The maximum pressure in the pipe..

 Velocity       -   The speed of the water moving through the                                                                                                                                                                                                           pipe.

 

3) SELECTION

First select the standard. You may either set the standard equal to the value that the element has been design for or enter your own value.

Note that pipes do not have design standards for flow and velocity. In this case, the only option is to input your own standard in the Value box.

 

4) CRITERIA

After having set the item and attribute to be checked based on the selection value, you must now specify the range to be displayed. Irri Maker will process all the items complying to the criteria specification.

 

 

5a) DISPLAY

Based on the above selection, Irri Maker will scan all the items and display the items complying to the parameters, in the standard ID colour. (See Settings - Colours - Other colours)

 

5b) REPORT

This command will also check all the criteria and print the results into the editor. Select if all the data or only the ones complying to the selection must be reported. You can also specify that the data must be reported in a sorted format.

 

Example: To see which of the nodes do not fall within 10% of their design pressure.  On the Items select Nodes. In Check, set Pressure. Set the SELECTION to Design and the CRITERIA percentage to 10%. Now check the WITHIN radio button.

 

 

Only active elements:

If this is checked, only elements that are part of the active water path will be displayed.

DTM points

The command in this box will copy the items as selected in the ITEM and CHECK boxes to the DTM.

EXAMPLE: To draw contours of the pressure values of all the nodes.                

Set the ITEMS to Nodes and the CHECK to Pressure. Irri Maker will generate new dtm points in the selected project (1,2 or 3). The elevation of these points will have the pressure at the node as value. Once the points have been copied, use the regular DTM functions (Triangle, contours) to display the data. A 3D can also be produced of the model.

 

 

EU and DU

 

Three values related to the eu and du are displayed.

 

The first eu value uses the following formula

 EU% = 50*((Ave/Max)+(Min/Ave))

 The discharge of each emitter is determined and an average is calculated. The emitter with the lowest flow is calculated and so also for the highest flow.

 The formula then calculates the EU as (average flow/maximum flow) + (minimum flow/average flow).

 

The du is calculated as follows.

 DU% =  (qavlq/qav)*100

         qavlq        = average discharge of emitters in the lowest quarter.

         qav        = average of all the flows.

 

The second eu calculation is more comprehensive and takes the number of plants and manufacturers cv value into account.

 EU% = {1-(1.27*Cv/n^.5)}*qavlq/qav

 This is similar to the du calculation above except that the answer is modified by the number of plants and cv value in the formula.
