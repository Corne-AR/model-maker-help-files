MENU : Vol - Damvol                                

 

Calculate the volume of a mine dump by calculating the area of all the contours and then applying the end area method to determine the volume. Calculating the volume of a dam is done in the same manner. However, a dam has a full capacity level and the contours should not be calculated above that value.

 

This function is modified to allow you to supply the over flow level. All the contours below that level will be calculated and the surface area at the full water mark will be added. A cumulative report of the area of all the contours will be printed to the editor.

 

Note that this method adds up the areas of all the contours and cannot determine whether a contour holds water or not. It is your responsibility to manipulate the triangle model to obtain the desired result.

 

1) Down stream

If the dam is in a valley, make sure that the triangles at the down stream side of the dam wall are deleted. The triangles modeling this part of the wall are not part of the water surface and must not be used.

 

EXAMPLE:

A contour plan of a dam in a natural valley. The contours on the down stream side of the dam wall are not removed. This would yield an invalid volume.

 

The same model with the undesired triangles removed. The wipeout functions were used to obtain the result. This model will calculate the correct answer.

The same principal applies to any dam that has triangles to the outside of the dam wall.

 

2) Islands

This function is not suitable to calculate a volume of a dam if it has any islands within the water surface area. To obtain the correct result, the area of these islands should be subtracted from the total water volume. This function is not designed to distinguish between regular contours and contours modeling an island and as such will yield a too high water volume.

 

The correct way to calculate complicated models like that would be to use the TERRACE functions.

 

Load the regular model of the dam in project # 1. Create a dummy model in project #2 with 4 points. The elevations of these points should be exactly at the full water mark level. The 4 points should also be placed far enough apart for the triangles formed between them to cover the dam model in total.

 

Alternatively, use the flat surface option in the Terrace to simulate the full water mark.

         

If a Terrace Grid volume is calculated between the 2 models Model Maker will calculate the cut & fill volume. Islands in the water will not add to the fill volume and islands higher than the water level will be calculated as a cut volume.

         

The water capacity of the dam will then be determined by the fill volume of the calculation.

 

EXAMPLE:

Figure 1

In the figure above, a dam with an island is modeled. The position of the dam wall is shown as well the position of a long section (profile) through the island, basin and wall.

Points a,b,c,d are the 4 points added to project 2 and represent the surface area and level of the water.

Note that points b and d are strategically placed over the dam wall so as not to generate any fill volume below the dam wall.

Note the cut areas above the water level. The fill area indicates where the water will be.

Note the tip of the island above the water is calculated as cut. Also the surrounding ground surface above the water level is regarded as a cut volume. The white crosses show the fill area and the fill volume would be the amount of water the dam can hold.

  DEPTH m           CUT m3      FILL m3

   0.00 to  1.00   :    1359.99        1998.86

   1.00 to  2.00   :     619.54         1047.82

   2.00 to  3.00   :      96.38           383.42

   3.00 to  4.00   :       0.69             31.42

 

FINAL CUT  AREA :  1788.00m2  |  CUT  VOLUME :  2076.59m3

FINAL FILL AREA :  2512.00m2   |  FILL VOLUME :  3461.51m3

 

The table of depth intervals relates to the amount of volume taken in horizontal slices from the design surface (water level) downwards.

The cut volume must be ignored in total as it refers to the surface of the model above the water level.

The fill volume is equivalent to the water volume.

 

Once the volume is calculated, Model Maker can print a report of the cumulative volumes per contour elevation. Use this printout to determine the dam capacity at different stages.
