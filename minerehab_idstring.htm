MINE : Rehab - Id String

 Top  Previous  Next

 

This rehab function is specifically designed to automate and optimize the cut & fill volume calculations when filling an excavated ramp to user defined maximum slope standards.

The type of ramps referred to here, are mostly found when big draglines are used to excavate coal in an open pit mining operation.

 

It is normally required that the water should drain out of the ramp, that an optional ditch must be formed along the center alignment of the ramp and that the side slopes may not be steeper than a predefined value. The purpose of this function is to develop a new surface that will satisfy the parameter requirements but also do it with the least amount of material movement and also in the end, have a balanced cut and fill volume distribution.

 

This rehab method works with the following elements:

1.Gridded DTm data. If your survey data is in a random format, use 3D grid option to create points on a regular grid.
2.String. At least 2 strings are needed. One string to define the centerline of the ramp and another to define the toe point positions of the centerline string.

To define the strings, do the following:.

 

a) Draw a general string along the center of the ramp. Use the String, vertical view function and grade the centerline string according to the required slope to drain the ramp (after it is rehabilitated).

 

 

 

 

b) Draw parallel strings (2) to the center string (1) if a flow ditch must be formed in the middle.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

c) To add the toeline string, use the Toeline function and add strings (3 & 4) on both sides of the new rehab surface.

 

 

Since the center strings will normally be above the ramp surface, one has to select the Force Toepoints Up option. This will force the toeline strings be higher than the centerline and thus form a V slope towards the middle.

 

These strings will hit the sides and define (with the centreline strings) the surface of the Fill area.

 

 

 

 

 

 

Important.

 

When the left and right toeline strings (3 & 4) have been, they exist as 2 separate, individual strings. These 2 strings must be combined so they can define the total area that must be rehabilitated.

 

This is done by using the String, Edit, Combine function.

Rehabilitation

 

The setup work has now been done and the Rehab option can be used.

 

1)  Now use the Mining, Rehab, Rehab-String option.  (the function of this help topic)

 

The following dialog boxes are shown:

 

 Confirm the grid spacing.        If the survey points of the active dtm project is not on a regular grid, the function will not proceed.

 

 Select the Identified Strings option.

 

When asked to indicate the boundary string, point to the toe string (the one combined from strings 3 & 4)

 

 

Rehab parameters

 

1) Maintain balanced volume

 

 

1) All the side slopes (along the toeline string) will be cut to at least this maximum slope.

 

2) After all the side slopes have been cut to the maximum and still more material is needed, Model Maker will start cutting the slopes (closest to where the material is needed) until enough material is gained or the minimum slope is reached.

 

3) Enter the preferred maximum hauling distance. Model Maker will try to complete the cut & fill design without moving material further than the maximum. This is not always possible and if cut material is not available within the maximum radius, the iterations will eventually stop with a deficit of cut material.

 

There are 3 stages in the rehab process.

 

1) MM cuts all the side slopes to the preferred (maximum) outside slope and the amount of material is recorded. In the same go, the program also determines how much fill is needed to excavate the model to the surface as defined by the identified string. These 2 values are displayed.

 

2) The available cut (from maximum slopes) is then distributed in to the ramp. Material is moved along the closest distance from cut to fill and the ramp is filled to the ID string levels.

 

If the available cut is more than the fill needed, the ramp will be filled and the side slopes cut. This will then result in a model that is not going to balance and in practice, the excess cut material will have to placed somewhere else.

 

If the Cut is less than the fill, stage 3 is processed.

 

3) Model Maker will make repeated sweeps through the ramp and systematically cut the sides to flatter slopes to gain material where it is needed in the ramp.

 

A running total is displayed at the bottom

 

Once the ramp is completely filled, the user can select  either undo the rehab or have the new elevations applied to the model.

 

2) As is cut and fill

 

Model maker will use the identified strings and update the surface elevations accordingly.

 

Use this method if you have designed the new model in total by means of the strings. That is, both the cut and fill elevations are derived from the strings.
