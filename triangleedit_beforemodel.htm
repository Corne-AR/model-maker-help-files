MENU : Tri - Edit

 

This is the common method used by most DTM programs. The method is functional and useful but, like most other DTM programs, tedious if it cannot be combined with manual editing methods.

 

Load the survey points and go to the  CAD  menu (if available, otherwise the actual lines could not be drawn). Finalize the CAD drawing by joining all the points that were measured as breaklines. This will result in CAD lines indicating all the breaklines as surveyed in the field. This method relies on your understanding of the terrain or that the surveyor's coding system is comprehensive. After all the breaklines are drawn, use the  ID  function in CAD to identify the lines. Now go back to the  TRI  menu and use the  PAR  function.

 

 

Activate the  CAD ID LINE SEARCH  ( )button.

 

This instructs  Model Maker to search within the  ID,d CAD lines when new triangles are formed. All the triangles formed in this way will have no legs crossing the CAD lines but all will lie upon the CAD lines.

 

Note:

Only the normal search option takes IDENTIFIED CAD lines into account. All of the above have no influence on the  QUICK SEARCH  method.

 

After the  NORMAL SEARCH is complete, you are the process in control again and can add, edit or delete triangles manually.
