MENU : Example - Getting started

 

Model Maker is a comprehensive package that produce the simplest contour plans as well as many more complicated tasks such as open cast mine planning exercises.

 

The program is an open system, allowing you to customize the options to suit your project and suit your personal style.

Model Maker was designed to offer maximum flexibility and often no two Model Maker users will handle a specific exercise in exactly the same way.

 

This section and the working examples will show you how the program operates. Feel free to experiment with all the other options as well.

 

Model Maker uses the following elements:

1) DTM                                points consisting of the co-ordinates and elevations.

2) Triangles                to model the surface based on the DTM points.

3) CAD                                elements to produce the drawing.

4) Strings                to supplement the drawing or assist in the design.

5) Irrigation                systems design.

 

DTM data is inputted using:

1) Files

2) Electronic devices

3) Digitizing

4) DXF files

5) Manual entering.

 

Triangles are created onto the DTM points in the triangle menu. Triangles may be saved andloaded from disk.

 

CAD can be generated in the following ways:

1) CAD menu.

2) Semi automatically.

3) From DXF.

 

Strings are generated in the GXT menu
